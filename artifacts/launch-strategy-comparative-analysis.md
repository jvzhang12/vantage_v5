# Launch Strategy: Comparative Analysis

Base Workspace: thanksgiving-holiday
Question: Which launch strategy—conservative rollout, focused MVP, or aggressive launch—is best for our new software product, given their assumptions, effects, and risks?

## Shared Context
The team must select one of three distinct launch strategies for a new software product. Each approach differs in scope, speed, and risk, and should be chosen in light of explicit assumptions about resources, market readiness, and tolerance for uncertainty. As Vantage's planning reasoning emphasizes, exploring these as parallel branches allows side-by-side evaluation based on their likely outcomes and fallback options.

## Shared Assumptions
- The product is near launch-ready but unproven in the market.
- Initial resources (staff, budget, support) are limited but not negligible.
- User feedback and market response will be critical to long-term success.
- Thanksgiving and the holiday season are relevant for timing due to increased user activity and heightened support needs.

## Branches Compared
- thanksgiving-holiday--conservative-rollout
- thanksgiving-holiday--focused-mvp
- thanksgiving-holiday--aggressive-launch

## Summary
Each strategy offers a balance of speed, learning, and risk control. Conservative rollout prioritizes stability but may lose momentum. Focused MVP aims for fast learning and modest risk but depends on careful targeting. Aggressive launch maximizes early exposure and growth but risks significant backlash if problems occur, especially during seasonal peaks.

## Tradeoffs
- Conservative rollout reduces catastrophic risk but limits growth and may miss seasonal opportunities.
- Focused MVP accelerates feedback and can optimize feature set iteratively but risks alienating users if scope is too small or niche is misjudged.
- Aggressive launch captures maximum attention and potential upside, but risks large-scale failure at a critical time if mismanaged.

## Recommendation
Adopt the Focused MVP approach: Launch to a well-defined target group with essential features. This approach balances feedback speed and risk, allowing for learning and feature iteration before broadening rollout—especially important in the volatile, high-support holiday window.

## Next Steps
- Identify and profile the MVP target user group.
- Prioritize feature set for the narrowest viable scope.
- Develop contingency and support plans for holiday surge.
- Establish clear metrics for evaluating MVP success and readiness to scale.
