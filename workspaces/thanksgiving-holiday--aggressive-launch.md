# Scenario: Full-feature, large-scale market release aiming for maximum visibility and growth from day one.

Base Workspace: thanksgiving-holiday
Question: Which launch strategy—conservative rollout, focused MVP, or aggressive launch—is best for our new software product, given their assumptions, effects, and risks?
Status: Counterfactual Branch

## Shared Context
The team must select one of three distinct launch strategies for a new software product. Each approach differs in scope, speed, and risk, and should be chosen in light of explicit assumptions about resources, market readiness, and tolerance for uncertainty. As Vantage's planning reasoning emphasizes, exploring these as parallel branches allows side-by-side evaluation based on their likely outcomes and fallback options.

## Shared Assumptions
- The product is near launch-ready but unproven in the market.
- Initial resources (staff, budget, support) are limited but not negligible.
- User feedback and market response will be critical to long-term success.
- Thanksgiving and the holiday season are relevant for timing due to increased user activity and heightened support needs.

## Preserved Assumptions
- Market window and first-mover momentum matter greatly.
- Support and ops can handle high initial load.
- Substantial marketing budget and launch resources are available.

## Changed Assumptions
- All major product risks have been sufficiently mitigated pre-launch.

## Predicted First-Order Effects
- Rapid user growth possible.
- Maximum data and market feedback immediately.
- Brand exposure (positive or negative) is amplified.

## Predicted Second-Order Effects
- If problems surface, damage is widespread.
- Holiday season offers a growth opportunity but intensifies demand for stability/support.
- Competitors may be deterred by strong entrance—or may exploit visible weaknesses.

## Risks
- Major bugs or support gaps could damage brand irreversibly.
- Overextension of resources (support, ops, dev, marketing).
- Difficulty reversing or pivoting under public scrutiny.

## Open Questions
- Is the product truly robust enough for this scale?
- Can support channels absorb holiday surge if demand spikes?

## Confidence
Moderate for high growth, low for risk containment.
