# Scenario: Gradual, region- or segment-limited release with controlled feedback loops.

Base Workspace: thanksgiving-holiday
Question: Which launch strategy—conservative rollout, focused MVP, or aggressive launch—is best for our new software product, given their assumptions, effects, and risks?
Status: Counterfactual Branch

## Shared Context
The team must select one of three distinct launch strategies for a new software product. Each approach differs in scope, speed, and risk, and should be chosen in light of explicit assumptions about resources, market readiness, and tolerance for uncertainty. As Vantage's planning reasoning emphasizes, exploring these as parallel branches allows side-by-side evaluation based on their likely outcomes and fallback options.

## Shared Assumptions
- The product is near launch-ready but unproven in the market.
- Initial resources (staff, budget, support) are limited but not negligible.
- User feedback and market response will be critical to long-term success.
- Thanksgiving and the holiday season are relevant for timing due to increased user activity and heightened support needs.

## Preserved Assumptions
- Resources are sufficient for slow, careful scaling.
- Early issues can be contained to small user populations.
- It's acceptable to forgo rapid initial growth in favor of stability.

## Changed Assumptions
- Lower short-term revenue/growth is tolerable.

## Predicted First-Order Effects
- Lower risk of catastrophic failure.
- Limited brand impact if issues arise.
- Slower accumulation of user feedback.
- Higher ability to iterate and patch before scaling.

## Predicted Second-Order Effects
- Slower word-of-mouth and market penetration.
- Competitors may enter or outpace during ramp-up.
- Might miss holiday season momentum.

## Risks
- Loss of market share to faster-moving rivals.
- Demoralization from slow results.
- Resource drain if scaling stays too slow.

## Open Questions
- Will slow feedback loops delay critical pivots?
- Does waiting provide real benefit, or just lost opportunity?

## Confidence
High for stability, low for fast gains.
