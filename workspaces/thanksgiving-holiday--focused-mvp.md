# Scenario: Limited-feature (minimum viable product) release aimed at a target user group for rapid validation.

Base Workspace: thanksgiving-holiday
Question: Which launch strategy—conservative rollout, focused MVP, or aggressive launch—is best for our new software product, given their assumptions, effects, and risks?
Status: Counterfactual Branch

## Shared Context
The team must select one of three distinct launch strategies for a new software product. Each approach differs in scope, speed, and risk, and should be chosen in light of explicit assumptions about resources, market readiness, and tolerance for uncertainty. As Vantage's planning reasoning emphasizes, exploring these as parallel branches allows side-by-side evaluation based on their likely outcomes and fallback options.

## Shared Assumptions
- The product is near launch-ready but unproven in the market.
- Initial resources (staff, budget, support) are limited but not negligible.
- User feedback and market response will be critical to long-term success.
- Thanksgiving and the holiday season are relevant for timing due to increased user activity and heightened support needs.

## Preserved Assumptions
- Fast, honest feedback is vital.
- It's possible to support a narrow use case or audience at launch.
- Some feature deferral is acceptable.

## Changed Assumptions
- Early adopters are willing to accept (and tolerate) an incomplete product.

## Predicted First-Order Effects
- Accelerated learning from a real user base.
- Lower build/support burden initially.
- Can iterate or pivot quickly based on real data.

## Predicted Second-Order Effects
- Early users may become evangelists—or critics.
- Easier to manage resource-wise, but risk of negative perception if the MVP falls short.
- Rapid iteration can help win market share if executed well.

## Risks
- MVP perceived as 'too minimal,' harming reputation.
- Lack of holiday-specific features could limit relevance.
- Feedback from a niche may not generalize to full market.

## Open Questions
- Does target user group reflect broader market needs?
- How soon can the product scale beyond MVP if the response is positive?

## Confidence
Moderate: balances risk and speed, but rests on the right MVP targeting.
